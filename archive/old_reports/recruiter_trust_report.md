# Recruiter Trust Report: redrob-ranker

## Executive Summary

redrob-ranker is a deterministic, evidence-based system that ranks 100,000 candidates for an ML/Information Retrieval role. The system is designed to be transparent, auditable, and human-in-the-loop, providing recruiters with actionable insights they can trust and verify.

---

## Why a Recruiter Would Trust This System

### Evidence-Based Scoring (75% Weight)

The ranking is grounded in verifiable career history and actual candidate qualifications, not opaque model predictions. The scoring formula weights evidence at 75%, meaning the majority of every candidate's score comes directly from facts that can be independently verified.

### Transparent Reasoning

Every candidate receives a grounded explanation that cites specific evidence from their profile. There are no black-box scores. Recruiters can read the reasoning behind any ranking and verify it against the candidate's actual history.

### Anomaly Detection

Suspicious profiles are flagged automatically:
- **High confidence anomalies**: Excluded from final rankings (e.g., profiles with AI-buzzword-only content)
- **Medium confidence anomalies**: Penalized in scoring but not excluded, allowing for human judgment

## Trust Signals

Every candidate in the output receives a detailed explanation that cites actual facts from their career history. There are no generic or fabricated justifications.

The system flags profiles with suspicious patterns:
- **High confidence**: Profile excluded from rankings (e.g., AI-generated buzzword dumps with no substance)
- **Medium confidence**: Profile penalized but included, flagged for human review

This ensures recruiters can focus on genuine candidates while reviewing flagged profiles with appropriate skepticism.

---

## What the System Does NOT Do

### No External APIs or Hosted Inference

All processing runs locally. No candidate data is sent to external services. This ensures data privacy and eliminates external dependencies.

### No Hallucinated Candidate Qualifications

The system never invents skills, experiences, or qualifications that aren't present in the candidate's profile. If a candidate didn't work at a company, the system won't claim they did.

### No Sole Reliance on Keyword Matching

While keywords are part of the evidence extraction, the system uses structured parsing and evidence-based scoring to avoid the pitfalls of naive keyword matching (e.g., rejecting qualified candidates who use different terminology).

---

## Risk Mitigation

### Coarse Filter First

An initial coarse filter removes obviously irrelevant candidates before detailed scoring. This reduces noise and ensures the ranking focuses on potentially qualified candidates.

### Negative Pattern Detection

AI-buzzword-only profiles—those that list trendy ML terms without evidence of actual experience—are caught and flagged. These profiles typically lack concrete projects, verifiable work history, or technical depth.

### Evidence-Only Baseline

The baseline scoring model preserves interpretability. Every score can be traced back to specific evidence items. This makes the system auditable and debuggable.

---

## Recommendations for Production Use

### Always Pair with Human Review

The system is a decision-support tool, not a decision-maker. Final hiring decisions should always involve human judgment.

### Use Anomaly Flags as Discussion Starters

Flagged profiles (medium confidence) should be reviewed with appropriate context. Some may be false positives—candidates with unusual career paths that don't fit standard patterns but are still qualified.

### Top-50 Candidates Are Ready for Outreach

The top 50 candidates demonstrate strong-to-moderate fit and are suitable for immediate recruiter outreach. The system's ranking is reliable enough to prioritize this pipeline.

## Conclusion
